POST 1

You Don’t Have a Traffic Problem. You Have a Leak Problem.

If people are viewing your listings but not buying, Etsy isn’t “hiding you.” Your listing is failing to convert attention into confidence.

Here’s the pattern I see daily: views → favorites → maybe carts → silence. That’s not bad luck. That’s a broken decision pathway. Buyers clicked… but your listing didn’t finish the job.

What leaks usually look like:

  • Photos don’t answer questions fast (buyers stay uncertain).
  • Offer isn’t obvious (what’s included? what do I choose?).
  • Trust is buried (reviews/policies/processing time hidden).
  • Info order is wrong (buyers do mental work instead of flowing).

A professional audit doesn’t “add more tactics.” It removes friction, strengthens certainty, and makes your existing traffic finally pay you.

POST 2

Ranking Isn’t Revenue. Persuasion Is Revenue.

You can show up in search and still make zero sales if the listing doesn’t create certainty. Visibility gets you clicked. Structure gets you paid.

Every listing must win a sequence of micro-decisions. Fail one step, and the buyer exits — not because your product is bad, but because your listing made the decision feel unsafe.

The 5 decisions every buyer makes:

  1. Click — relevance at a glance.
  2. Scroll — momentum through images.
  3. Understand — clarity without rereading.
  4. Trust — proof without hunting.
  5. Buy — low-risk, high-confidence decision.

Most sellers only optimize step one (keywords + titles). A real audit optimizes the entire buying journey — because that’s where the money lives.

POST 3

Etsy Isn’t Random. Your Changes Are.

Sellers change titles today, photos tomorrow, prices next week… then call Etsy “unstable.” That’s not instability. That’s noise.

Etsy performance is multi-variable. When you change too many things, you destroy diagnosis. You can’t tell what worked, what harmed conversion, or what brought the wrong buyer.

Why DIY optimization backfires:

  • New keywords bring wrong intent → clicks rise, sales fall.
  • New photos reduce clarity → scroll happens, trust fails.
  • New price triggers hesitation → favorites increase, purchases drop.

An audit creates a clean fix order: highest leak first, one controlled change at a time. That’s how you build stable growth instead of emotional editing.

POST 4

The Algorithm Isn’t Punishing You. Buyers Are Voting “No.”

Etsy watches buyer behavior. Clicks without purchases is a silent signal: “This listing didn’t deliver certainty.”

If buyers enter your listing and exit, Etsy learns that your listing isn’t satisfying intent. That’s why a listing can “rank” today and fade tomorrow — even if you didn’t do anything wrong.

What Etsy reads as quality:

  • Click-through (interest)
  • Engagement (attention)
  • Cart adds (consideration)
  • Purchases (satisfaction)

The fastest way to “fix ranking” is to fix hesitation inside the listing. Audit the human decision first — and the algorithm follows the improvement.
